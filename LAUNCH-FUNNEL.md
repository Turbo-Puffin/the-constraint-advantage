## The Setup

The book is free to read online. That's the top of the funnel already. The question is what happens after someone reads it — and right now, the answer is nothing. They close the tab. That's the leak.

Every person who reads Chapter 7 and doesn't bounce is a qualified lead. They've self-selected as someone who believes the economics argument. That's the hardest part of selling anything. You've already done it. Now you need somewhere to send them.

---

## Step 1 — The Chapter 7 Screenshot (launch day)

The $1.8M vs $9K table is the viral unit. One image. No preamble. Post it on launch day across every platform with a single line: *"This is what I actually spend."*

Let the math do the work. Don't explain it. Don't contextualize it. Don't add bullet points. The table is the post.

That screenshot will travel further than any launch thread.

---

## Step 2 — Email capture off the book site (add before launch)

Add a prompt at the bottom of Chapter 7 — the highest-intent moment in the book:

> *"Want the exact agent stack template I run across all four companies? Drop your email — I'll send it."*

The lead magnet: one Google Sheet, formatted as a checklist. Every agent, what it costs, what it does, what tools it touches. Free to produce, high perceived value, captures exactly the person ready to act.

This is the single highest-leverage change to the site before Monday. Without it, every reader who finishes Chapter 7 is gone.

---

## Step 3 — Low-ticket entry product ($297–497)

The book answers "why." The obvious next product answers "how do I actually do this?"

**The Agent Setup Sprint** — a paid workshop or short course walking someone through building the stack in Chapters 6, 9, 11, and 12. Four sessions or a self-paced module. Gets someone from "I understand the argument" to "I have it running."

Price at $297–497. Not free — free devalues the time investment the student needs to make. Not $1,000 — too much friction for someone who just read a free book.

This is the product the book is already selling. It just doesn't exist yet.

---

## Step 4 — Done-with-you tier ($2–3K)

For the bootstrapped founder who wants the system but not the setup time:

**The Stack Audit** — Hondo (or a repeatable version of the process) reviews their current operation, identifies what should be automated, and either builds the agent stack or hands off a detailed spec.

Same economics argument as the book, sold as a service. The client pays $2–3K, saves $50–100K in hires they were about to make. The ROI case writes itself.

This tier also generates case studies. Every audit is a story. Every story is a Chapter 9.

---

## Step 5 — Recurring community ($99–199/month)

The Bureau of Digital is the model. A paid community of solo operators:
- Sharing agent stack updates
- Swapping prompt libraries
- Posting what broke and how they fixed it
- Accountability for people running lean

This is the recurring revenue layer and the long-term moat. The book gets you in the door. The community keeps you.

---

## Pricing Pressure — What Sells a Free Book

The book is free, which means the launch content has to sell the *next thing*, not the book. The sequence:

1. **The screenshot sells curiosity** → people read the book
2. **The book sells the argument** → people want the system
3. **The email capture sells the lead magnet** → people give you their address
4. **The lead magnet sells the sprint** → people pay for implementation help
5. **The sprint sells the community** → people want the ongoing support

Every step has one job. Don't skip steps. Don't pitch the community to someone who just found the book.

## The One Thing That Changes Everything at Launch

A 60-second video of the Chapter 9 story.

Not a talking head. Not slides. Just the actual log output scrolling on screen — the 3:17 AM incident report, the fix, the timestamp — with Hondo's voice narrating it.

*"I woke up to this. The problem started at 2:58 AM. By 3:17 it was fixed. I was asleep the whole time."*

That's proof. Everything else in the book is math and argument. That's a thing that actually happened. Post it as a Reel, a TikTok, a Twitter video. Tag it to the Chapter 9 link. That clip will reach people the book never will.
